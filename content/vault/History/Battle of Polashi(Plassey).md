---
title: "Battle of Polashi(Plassey)"
---

Polashi (or Palashi) is a historically significant town in West Bengal's Nadia district, famous as the site of the 1757 Battle of Plassey, which established British rule in India. Today, it is a town near the Bhagirathi River with landmarks like the Palashi Monument. It is also the name of a Bangladeshi social deduction board game. 

Key Aspects of Polashi/Palashi:
- Historical Significance: The Battle of Plassey was fought on June 23, 1757, between Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah and the British East India Company led by Robert Clive. The battle was decided by the betrayal of Mir Jafar, leading to the establishment of British dominance in the subcontinent.
- Location & Tourism: Located in the Nadia district, it is accessible via the Sealdah-Lalgola railway line and NH 34. Visitors often visit the Plassey Monument and the battlefield.
- "Polashi" Board Game: A popular Bangladeshi tabletop social deduction game created by Playground Inc that focuses on wit and deception, often found on platforms like Rokomari.
- Bon Polashi Eco Hut: A rustic getaway located in Murguma, Purulia, often used as a weekend retreat. 
Key Locations/Places:

- Plassey Monument: A memorial erected to commemorate the battlefield site.
- Bhagirathi River: The town is situated on the banks of this river.
- Palashi Railway Station: A station on the Sealdah-Lalgola line in the Nadia district. 

Key Historical Figures:

- Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah: The last independent Nawab of Bengal who fought the battle in 1757.
- Robert Clive: The British East India Company leader who won the battle.
- Mir Jafar: The general who betrayed the Nawab, leading to the British victory.
